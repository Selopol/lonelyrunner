A LIVE MATHEMATICS EXPERIMENT

Can AI make the 14th runner lonely?

Between 2025 and 2026, computers proved that each of 13 runners on a circular track eventually runs alone. The case of 14 has never been settled. A frontier model and one ten-core machine are attacking it here, in public. Every step below is a hash-chained artifact.

The problem

∃t :  ‖vi t‖ ≥ 1⁄14  for all i

You stand still on a circular track of length one. Thirteen runners pass you forever, each at a different constant speed. The conjecture, posed by Jörg Wills in 1967, says a moment must come when every runner is at least 1/14 of the track away from you: a moment when you are lonely. Nobody has proven that this moment exists. For speeds (1, 2, …, 13) it barely does; the margin is exactly 1/14, and not a hair more. That fragility is the whole difficulty.

The last six cases fell to one computational method: reduce the conjecture to a finite check over prime moduli, then make the check tractable. The record holders publish their code. Ours is a pinned fork of it.

The experiment

Three tracks run at once. Nothing on this page is simulated: if a number is quiet, the lab is quiet.

I The Hunt

Adversarial search for extremal speed configurations at the frontier. Candidates survive a numeric screen, then a certificate in exact rational arithmetic. A certified δ below 1/14 would disprove the conjecture outright. A certified δ equal to 1/14 is a tight configuration, the rarest object in this landscape.

II The Brain

The record holders name their bottleneck for 14 runners: the initial sieve I(k,p,1) explodes. A frontier model attacks that bottleneck in public: hypothesis, attempted lemma, counter-test against the solved cases, measured speedup or honest failure. The notebook is in the repository.

III The Backbone

The record method, running on our hardware. Solved cases reproduce in seconds to hours and anchor every claim we make; bounded probes into the 14-runner case measure the wall we are trying to breach. On one machine the full case would take years. We publish wall clocks, not promises.

Do not trust us

  • The solver is a fork of the code that set the 13-runner record, pinned at upstream 755b116. Diff it.
  • Every δ we publish is certified in exact rational arithmetic. The verifier and its regression against six known tight values from the literature are 80 lines of Python. Run them.
  • The journal is append-only and hash-chained from a genesis block. Raw solver logs are linked from every run. Recompute the chain.
  • We do not claim we will solve it. We claim that every number on this page is real.
