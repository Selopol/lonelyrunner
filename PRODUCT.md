# PRODUCT.md — The Lonely Runner Experiment

register: brand

## What this is

A live mathematics experiment with a public, hash-chained journal. An AI
(Claude Fable 5) and a 10-core Apple M4 attack the open frontier of the
Lonely Runner Conjecture: 14 runners, unsolved since 1967. The site is the
window into that experiment. A memecoin ($LONELY, ticker TBD) launches on
top of the experiment; the experiment is real either way.

## Users

- Crypto degens riding the July 2026 "AI breaks conjectures" wave (Alpöge +
  Fable 5 Jacobian counterexample, 2026-07-19). They need one glance to get
  it: real math, live, verifiable.
- Math-curious lurkers who click through from X. They stay for the circle.
- Skeptics and mathematicians who will try to catch us faking. They get raw
  logs, exact rational certificates, a pinned fork of the record-holders'
  code, and a hash chain. Every number on the page is an artifact.

## Brand voice

Three words: chalk-precise, nocturnal, unhurried.
The page speaks like a good seminar: confident, exact, no hype. We ask a
question in the headline, we never claim victory. Honesty IS the brand:
the fastest way to die on crypto twitter is one fake number.

## Tone rules

- The headline is a question: "Can AI make the 14th runner lonely?"
- Never "solving", always "attacking / measuring / hunting".
- Real metrics only: verified primes, certified deltas, sieve layer sizes.
  Absolutely no fake progress percentages.
- English only. No em dashes anywhere.

## Anti-references

- Neon-on-black crypto slop, laser eyes, rocket emojis.
- Paper-white academic minimalism (arXiv cosplay).
- SaaS hero-metric template, identical card grids, purple gradients.
- Any UI that animates fake activity when the journal is quiet.

## Strategic principle

The site is a window into the journal (journal/events.jsonl), nothing more.
If the journal is quiet, the site shows a quiet lab, honestly. Every visual
element traces to a journal event or a certified artifact in the repo.
